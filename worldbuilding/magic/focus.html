Focus

Focus, or the technique to maintain magic.

De facto only describing that mages need to concentrate on magic and use up "mental capacity" to keep effects alive.

Short lived magic often only requires a initial setup and barely any focus, often mages can keep focus just by being very trained or used to keeping certain magic up.

The amount of focus require thereby depends on general magic skill, mental fortitude, intuition and magic complexity.

Keeping focus is similar to memorizing any amount of abstract information.


Index

Most magic is very complex, this comes in contrast to how easily most mages handle their magic.

Lifelong training or an inborn talent and intuition for certain spheres is what allows mages to cast magic in a timely manner at all.

In order to manage the planning overhead, in particular with magic that has to be maintained longer than it's initial cast, mages use indexes.

As the name implies, an index contains the most pertinent information about a given magic effect, such as, but not limited to:

- amount and type of energy required to maintain

- shape of the form and the sphere it's cast in

- position and shape of sphere presence employed in casting, usually arcane

- function, purpose and additional notes (depending on preference)

Indexes are usually cast as meta-magic in addition with the base effect, interwoven with it, so when one disappears, the other does as well.


Indexes can take any shape and can even be invisible.

Given that they are supposed to be an aid for the mage, indexes are usually visible in the physical sphere, for highest comfort.

While the look of a given index is up to each mage, certain common practices have been established over time.


The most common form of index is a glowing disk of lights, using script to convey information on it's surface, with a hole in it's center.

This disk is usually floating and rotating around it's center, "worn" on a mages arm, horn or tail, whichever is convenient.


The script on indexes is readable to all, as normal script would be, it is thereby commonplace to invent personal shorthands or cyphers.

This is done to stop possible unwanted interaction with the magic through other mages.


Offloading

Given a reasonably sophisticated index, about any magic effect can be offloaded.

Offloading describes the technique to hand over magic effects to other mages or specifically prepared crystals.

Handing over magic is quite similar to how you would hand over any fragile construct in your hands to the hands of another.

Given a crystal that has been prepared / created for the purpose of being able to take over magic,

any suitably simple magic, or any magic with a complex enough index, can be offloaded to such a crystal.

Given the effect, the crystal will maintain and fuel the magic until the crystal is drained.
