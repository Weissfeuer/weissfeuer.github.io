Physical Plane

Also called the "plane of the body", the "flesh plane", the "normal plane" or the "force plane" to practitioners of force magic.

While not all creatures exist on the physical plane, it is very likely that you are one, and therefore will only encounter other creatures that also live there.

The physical plane works like you would expect with many of the rules according to the "real world".

It is the plane most creatures perceive easiest, the only sphere non-mages can see, and the plane most people assume to be the base of life.

The Physical is very low in magical energy, in contrast to all others, and most people assume you cannot cast magic in the physical plane at all.


Force Magic

Casting magic through such a low energy sphere is truly a feat, and something most people don't even think about.

Physical magic, or more commonly known as force magic, can be cast in two ways:


Firstly, the way it was discovered initially, through incredible bodily awareness and composure.

Mastering your own body and the way it reacts with the world around it.

The first "force mages", a term coined by a later group, were monks who practiced restraint and perfection of the body.

Their intent was not the use of such magic and this discovery came as a byproduct of their training.

These days people may want to train in this tradition, although practiced monks will still only think of it as a side-effect and not something to strive for.


The second group to discover force magic was a group of arcane mages wanting to imitate what the monks could do.

Force magic cast firstly through the arcane is much easier to learn and potentially more powerful, given the ease of access to arcane energy.


Bodily force magic is mainly "used" to enhance one's body, strengthening muscles or hardening skin.

It's main drawback are the difficulty of mastering it as well as it's short range, and thereby limited use.

Force magic is not precise and hard to maintain, even with training.

In combat it is best used to enhance one's body, deflect blows, create short-range "weapons or barriers.

Force barriers, while famous, are hard to create or maintain, usually requiring multiple mages to cooperate, even then only lasting seconds.

The advantages of force magic are few, related mostly to it being not well known and mostly a novelty.

The effects of force magic are almost invisible, both to the naked eye and to any arcane presence, since no arcane magic is used.

Any such effects can only be seen using the environment, such as rain or fog.


Absolute masters of force magic may be able to fully, but crudely control their own body, such as keeping a stopped heart beating.

Although any such effects require immense amounts of focus, it is best to search a healer quickly.


The arcane-fueled use of force magic is as mentioned previously easier and more powerful, with the drawback of the cast being visible to the arcane this way.

There isn't much use for such a imprecise and short-range magic usually, most mages that specialize in it do so for personal reasons mostly.

Directing "pure" force magic from the arcane, while possible, is far more costly and far less efficient than to use any other form of "impure" magic.

It is generally recommended to use Earth magic, for example, to create a barrier, than to use pure Force.
