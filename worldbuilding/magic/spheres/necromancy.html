Necromancy

Necromancy is a cover term for the magical use of soul and the creation of arcane constructs.

It is shunned in most societies for it's name-sake ability to "revive the dead".

Necromancy is however much more than that, and also more complicated than what a common person might know about it.


Use of Soul

What will get you branded as a necromancer really quickly is the use of soul.

The harvesting of soul is generally forbidden for large enough quantities of soul are only found in living beings.

Harvesting it then usually results in the death of the victim, or at least in their life-long suffering.

Any ability to interact with soul is therefore seen as a taboo by the general population.

Even healers that mend soul and want only good will keep their skills hidden, afraid of prosecution.


Soul is so valuable to necromancers because of it's immense energy potential, and it's easy availability if you have no scruples.

Necromancers need this energy to perform what they are usually most known for.


Artificial Life

Riving the dead, or so it seems, is the unholy art that sends shivers down the spine of any those that think about it.

Necromancers do in fact not create or revive the living.

The life they create is more akin to a machine, powered with soul, made from arcane magic.

The recently deceased are invaluable for this purpose.

It has nothing to do with any unholy energy ascribed to death, but rather because the bodies of the dead are ideal for the construction of arcane life.

A dead body has the same structures that it used to connect to it's spirit in life.

While quickly decaying, thus a fresh corpse being preferred, these structures can then be used as the baseline for the arcane network that will give the body fake life.

A necromancer thus avoids having to "re-invent the wheel" and can work from what supported life before.


Soul is therefore used to power this arcane construct, like it would any living being.

While it appears ironic and even nonsensical to steal soul from life just to power fake life, a construct like this is much more pliable to orders.


While it highly depends on the quality of the construct, in both the necromancer's skill and the material used, these minions are usually very dumb.

Only the inexperienced or foolish will actually try to create new life as it was before.

Those creations are more often than not stumbling, drooling husks.

Zombies and ghouls, feared by the public, but generally harmless, until their reservoir of soul runs dry.

Depending on the craftsmanship, a creature like that may just fall over, inert, when it's soul runs out.

Or it may decide to find new sources to sustain itself, shambling and with no regards what that soul might still be attached to.


No, any necromancer with any self respect will only create full controlled creatures.

These creations make take on any kind of shape, often even a gruesome mash of body parts.

"Good" necromancers, called white necromancers, will refuse to use corpses and instead work from a clean slate.

With this, they create things more akin to machines, animated by the arcane and soul, they can take on even more unconventional shapes.

They may range from a walking golem made of stone to hand-held objects with specific purpose.

White necromancers reject the title of necromancer, or accept it only begrudgingly, they prefer to compare their creations to glyphing.

And while many might disguise it like that, the stigma remains, any those that use the techniques of necromancy are shunned by society at large.


Power Reservoir

While most known, and feared, for their creations of "undeath", necromancers don't limit themselves to this use of soul.

In fact, many become necromancers only for potential that soul offers.

Such a "freely available" source of great power is sought by many, for any purpose the mind could come up with.

These mages will be necromancers secondarily only, their primary focus being in other practices.

A fire mage, looking for even greater power, might find themselves drawn into necromancy easily.

The raw destruction a fire mage could cause with only comparatively little soul is impressive, and alluring.

Other might seek soul for whatever other purpose, all they need is very little remorse.
