Void

Void magic is the result of a genetic mutation (or, rarer, mutilation) of a mage's arcane presence.

It can thereby not be learned, although similar effects can be achieved using regular arcane magic.


A change in structure of the arcane presence makes an affected mage unable to store and regulate magic energy, draining their own energies to sustain themselves.

Void-sick mages are however highly dependent on arcane energy, a lack of will cause the following (in order):

- Fatigue

- Inability to cast magic

- Additional damage to the arcane presence, as it is starting to self-cannibalize

- Physical damage to the mage's body

- Death


In order to counteract the constant drain on energy, a void mage will unwillingly siphon arcane energy from the environment.

This effect cannot be stopped by the mage themselves, only slowed or accelerated using focus.

Forcefully stopping this effect results in constant loss of energy and above described effects.

The rate of siphoning depends on the mage's current reserves, higher drain with lower levels of stored energy, and vice versa.


Issues

Given an environment with average energy levels, this is not a problem on it's own.

The issue lies with that face, that the mere presence of a void-sick mage drains energies around them.

This siphoning is undirected and can and will disrupt nearby magic forms and maintained effects, making it hard even for the mage themselves to cast magic.


Given this unpredictability, void mages are usually shunned by their social environment, even more so in societies that heavily rely on magic or employ complex magic.

In addition to that, using arcane sight, the arcane presence of affected mages appears sickening to most.

A void-sick presence is riddled with holes and tears, slightly distorting the arcane sphere around it, while draining energy.

Direct contact with the void presence will cause harm to other mage's presences, being slowly, if inefficiently, drained and consumed.

Even looking at such a presence can be painful depending on proximity to the void-presence.


Use as Magic

Void mages can learn to control their condition over time, while never being the master of it, as they rely on it for survival.

With enough experience, void magic can be directed slightly, most commonly for a close range disruption of magic.

In more grim ways, void magic can also be used to cripple other arcane presences, so as to stop mages from casting at all, up to permanently.

This doesn't result in more void mages, but will still cripple their arcane presence like you would cripple a limb.


More crafty void mages can make use of their condition in clever ways.

Their aptitude allows them to regenerate magic way faster than a normal mage would passively.

Emptying their reserves with normal magic, then siphoning with void allows for a high output of magic, given the mage is able to balance the flow such as to not cause self-harm.

A void mage might even want to deliberately keep their reserves low such as to cause more drain with void.

All of these techniques are dangerous if untrained and can harm the caster.
