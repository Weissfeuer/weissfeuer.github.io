Divine Magic

Ignorance

While it is hotly debated if there is a god, multiple or what kind of gods, it is hard to deny the effects divine powers have on the world.

The average person will pray and either have their prayer heard or not.

Religions and cults will rise and fall as divine interventions ebb and flow.


Precise knowledge of the divine sphere is available only to those that study it.

If fact, knowledge over the divine might be wholly undesirable.

Knowledge

The divine sphere is the 5th and last of the primary spheres.

It is both the sphere that people interact with the least and the most.


No conventional living being has a presence in the divine plane.

It is next to the arcane, and far surpassing it, the most volatile domain.

Having a presence in the divine is both hugely detrimental to the structural integrity of a living being, as well as a dark and dangerous potential.


The divine sphere can't be used to cast magic in any conventional sense.

Divine energy is by far the best source of power, much more than soul. It is however completely unusable due to the functions of the divine.

Truth

The divine is everyone's collective subconscious.

Every arcane mage will subconsciously create magic forms in the divine.

Letting the divine create effects from it by itself.

The sphere itself is incredibly susceptible to manipulations from the arcane.


A single mage's hopes and wishes, their fears and nightmares, judgement and hate. It all contributes to the divine.

But while a single person will not be enough to sway the storm in the divine, a group of people can.

A single shared idea, subconscious and unquestioned, followed with zeal. Such an idea will create an effect in the divine.


A village performing an age old ritual of sacrifice to bring the rain WILL bring the rain.

A prejudice held by many WILL come true.

Mass panic WILL make the reason of the fear become reality.

A cult leader directing their followers that their god will smite the unworthy, WILL call down the lightning.


Magic in the divine requires a strong will, a conviction and many people to believe in it.

Yet knowing of this, the working of the divine, makes you unable to contribute.

Once you realize how to manipulate the divine, you no longer can.

While you might still contribute with truly subconscious and unknown ideas, following rituals will no longer effect the divine for you.

You know how it works, you know what you are trying to do, what you want to achieve. And thereby you are denied.


Casting in the divine is thereby either impossible, or futile.

A divine mage never knows that they are one, to them they are religious followers, god's chosen, the will of the world, prophets.


The divine is thus both impractical to use and harmless, but also the most dangerous power to possibly exist.

False information, widespread prejudice, mass panics. A belief strong enough, precise enough and widespread enough will become reality.

The moons will crash down into the land if only enough people believe it will.


Further from such frightening, possible, yet unlikely events are cults.

A large enough group of people convinced in an idea, and following it with zeal and conviction, can cause divine effects almost predictably.

Those effects will usually be limited, the idea having to be both precise and backed by enough people without question.

A direct disbelief from an outsider working against it more than an average cultist might contribute even.

Thus a direction is needed, a leader to dictate and direct, telling the will of their god.

A cult leader can either be unaware of the workings of the divine, a true zealous follower like any other, thinking they are chosen by their god.

Or they can know about the divine, unable to contribute themselves, but directing the followers all the more, knowing how to crystalize their beliefs.

Such a leader would not need to follow their gods directions, as they are the god themselves, sometimes even directly to their followers.

What they proclaim will happen, because their followers already believe it will.


Thus is the spiral of the divine.

Belief causes divine effects, which causes stronger belief. "It was not just a belief, it really happened."

On the other hand, a wavering belief, dwindling numbers will make divine effects less and less likely, and the belief will disappear.
