Magic

Magic underlies rules, just like physics do in ((real life)).

It is separated into spheres (or domains), not just as a scholarly distinction, but by literally being different dimensions coexisting.

When talking about the dimension of a certain sphere, it may also be called a plane (of existence).


A typical person will have a body comprised of a physical, spirit and soul presence, while only being able to see physical with their eyes.

While many races are born with a natural aptitude for casting magic of certain spheres, as long as a person has a presence or "body" in that sphere they may learn to use magic from that sphere.


It is to be noted that in general, magic from one sphere cannot directly interact with magic or objects in another sphere. On the other hand, most objects exist in many spheres, by different presences, in addition to the fact that the arcane sphere intersects with other spheres quite heavily.

An example would be a fire spell, mostly comprised of arcane magic, still affecting wood, an object with no arcana presence. The magic in this case translates energy from the arcane into the physical, resulting in chaotic and often hard to control fire.


In order to cast magic, structure must be created in the desired sphere, with the desired form, to result in a magic effect. A more visual application is found in glyphing.


Primary spheres

Primary, or pure spheres, are actual sub-dimensions. They allow for bodies to exist within them and can spawn elementals, beings with a singular-sphere body.

Casting magic of a pure sphere is usually more difficult and less intuitive, but allows for greater control, as you cast magic closer to their base rules.

Primary spheres include the following:


Physical / Force

Spirit

Soul

Arcane

Divine


Secondary spheres

Secondary spheres are comprised of a mix of multiple primary spheres. They do not have sub-dimensions and cannot create elementals.

Strictly speaking, secondary spheres are more akin to conventions in magic use, rather than their own entities.

The common populous might call beings of multiple spheres "elementals", due to a lack of their magical understanding or formal education.

A fire "elemental" might be incorrectly called an elemental, because they seem to be compromised of only fire, yet they exist both in the Physical and the Arcane.


These spheres exist only as categories for the common folk and only vaguely describe their sphere-composition.

Additional Notes

Glyphing

The practice of inscribing magical structures onto physical objects, allowing non-mages to cast magic.

Crystals

Crystallized magical energy, stored for later use or as a reagent.

Focus

Object maintaining magic, or practice to offload maintaining magic onto a certain object.
