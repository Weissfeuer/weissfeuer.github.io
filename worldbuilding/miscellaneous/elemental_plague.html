Elemental Plague

Stories tell of time before the plague, at this point, it has become normalcy for many societies.

Just as ebb and flow, or the changing seasons, the plague is a force of nature to them.

Yet the observant warn, it was never part of nature and it has only gotten worse.


The elemental plague is a phenomenon in which areas, first small, then larger, become infected by destabilizing energy.

It will start small, an anomaly about the size of a pearl.

Any mage capable of even the slightest magic can re-stabilize this occurrence, leaving a small shard of magic crystal for the fortunate.


The real danger lies in what happens when the anomaly isn't found, if it can grow.

This destabilized matter will slowly, then with more volume faster, infect the area around it, growing in size steadily.

An anomaly out in the wilderness, or any unpopulated area will first be noticed by it's main symptom.

Elementals.

Not in the academic sense, mind you, these elementals are manifestations of chaotic energy, and will usually span most if not all spheres.

Mindless manifestations, a walking avatar of chaos, their form a barely coherent mass.

These elemental will usually be made from the underlying magical energies as well as any physical object it could suck up from it's vicinity.

A vortex of water and plant-life in a swamp, a living sandstorm in a desert, a hulking giant of razor-sharp leaves and wood in a forest.


In order to stop the growth of such an anomaly the elemental it produced has to be destroyed, the chaotic energies calmed.

While fighting such an elemental is no easy feat and usually requires multiple people, depending on it's size,

calming the anomaly can still be done by about any mage.

This however is not a complete removal.

An anomaly that has produced it's first elemental is never really destroyed, only delayed.

Depending on the anomaly's size and your personal luck, it may not produce another one for years.

In other cases they have grown so large, that they produce multiple elementals daily, and even their growth can't be halted outside of special procedures.


For many cultures is has become part of their daily life then, to go out, find and destroy elementals.

It has come so far in fact, the many will take the magic crystals the elementals leave behind as currency to trade with.

A service of protection against other wares.

Even if not to trade, such crystals, while chaotic, can still be used as any other crystal of it's quality would.

Some even use them as decoration for their intricate and unique patters.
